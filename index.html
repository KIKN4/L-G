    Frequently Asked Questions About Sealcoating

    What is sealcoating?

    Sealcoating is a protective layer applied to asphalt surfaces like driveways and parking lots. It helps protect the asphalt from damage caused by UV rays, water, oils, and general wear and tear.

    How long does sealcoating last?

    Typically, a professional sealcoating job can last 2-3 years, depending on traffic, weather conditions, and the quality of the application. Regular maintenance can extend its lifespan.

    What's the best time of year to sealcoat?

    The ideal time for sealcoating is during warm, dry weather. Late spring, summer, and early fall are typically the best seasons. The temperature should be at least 50°F (10°C) and rising.

    How often should I sealcoat my driveway?

    For residential driveways, we recommend sealcoating every 2-3 years. Commercial parking lots with higher traffic may need sealcoating every 1-2 years.

    Can sealcoating fix cracks in my asphalt?

    Sealcoating itself doesn't fix cracks. However, as part of our sealcoating process, we first repair any cracks or damage before applying the sealant. This comprehensive approach ensures the best protection for your asphalt.

    How long does the sealcoating process take?

    The duration depends on the size of the area, but most residential driveways can be completed in a day. Larger commercial projects may take several days. The surface then needs 24-48 hours to cure.

    Is sealcoating environmentally friendly?

    We use high-quality, water-based sealants that are more environmentally friendly than solvent-based options. These products have low VOC emissions and are safer for the environment.
